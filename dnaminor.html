DNA-minor

Introduction

These scripts show the structure of the complex between the DNA-binding protein PurR and its target DNA. PurR acts a repressor for several genes that make purines. (Based on 2pug.pdb, Lu et al. (1998) Biochemistry 37: 971-982)

DNA-minor 1

Here we see PurR bound to DNA. The dimeric protein (magenta and green ) binds to DNA via the N-terminal domain. The large C-terminal domain is responsible for dimer formation. The co-repressor, hypoxanthine (spacefill) stablizes dimer formation.

DNA-minor 2

The repressor binds to the major groove of the DNA through a helix-turn-helix motif (spacefill).

DNA-minor 3

In addition, another helix from each monomer binds to the minor groove.

DNA-minor 4

The interaction with the minor groove causes the minor groove to unwind, as shown by the space in the center of the groove.

DNA-minor 5

The DNA is also severely bent around the minor groove, as shown here. These structural changes are somehow responsible for silencing gene expression.
