Secondary Structure Motifs

Introduction

Proteins have a limited number of structural motifs.

Motif 1

This is a common secondary structure in proteins - the helix-loop-helix motif. (Based on 256B.pdb, (1981) J.Mol.Biol. 148: 427-448)

Motif 2

This helix-loop-helix motif is called the "EF HAND" and represents the CALCIUM -binding motif in many calcium-binding proteins (based on 5CPV.pdb, Swain et al. (1989) J.Biol.Chem. 264 : 16620-16628). The calcium ion is bound to two Asp side chains and a Glu side chain.

Motif 3

This is a hairpin beta-sheet motif. Note that the β-strands are anti-parallel (direction of the arrows).

Motif 4

This is the beta-alpha-beta motif. Note that the β-strands are parallel (direction of the arrows).
