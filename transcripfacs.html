Transcription Factors

Introduction

These scripts show how transcription factors interact with DNA.

Helix-Turn-Helix

One common motif for a DNA-binding protein is the helix- turn- helix. The recognition helix (DNA-binding helix) is green. (Based on 1lcd.pdb, Chuprina et al. (1993) J.Mol.Biol. 234: 446-462)

Leucine Zipper

This is a leucine zipper DNA-binding protein. Note how the two long alpha helices interact with the DNA. (Based on 1ysa.pdb, Ellenberger at al. (1992) Cell 71: 1223-1237)

Transcription Factors 3

In a leucine zipper DNA-binding protein the two helices are held together by hydrophobic interactions between, mainly, leucine sidechains .

Zinc-Finger

This is a zinc-finger DNA-binding protein. The zinc coordinates to the protein to create the DNA-binding helix . (Based on 1glu.pdb, Luisi et al. (1991) Nature 352: 497-505)

EcoRV

This is a restriction enzyme, EcoRV bound to its target DNA. (Based on 4rve.pdb, Winkler et al. (1993) EMBO J. 12 1781-1795)

Transcription Factors 6

Note how binding of EcoRV bends the DNA toward the major groove.
