Rhodopsin

Introduction

This script shows the structure of bacteriorhodopsin. Bacteriorhodopsin is a typical alpha helical membrane protein. The amphipathic helices in bacteriorhodopsin are also compared to those in a typical water soluble protein. (Based on 1brd.pdb, Henderson et al. (1990) J.Mol.Biol. 213: 899-929 and 1aep.pdb, Breiter et al. (1991) Biochemistry 30: 603-608)

Rhodopsin 1

This is bacteriorhodopsin from Halobacterium halobium. It is a transmembrane protein containing seven alpha helices.

Rhodopsin 2

Bacteriorhodopsin contains amphipathic helices in which the hydrophobic sidechains point outward so they can interact with the fatty acyl chains of the membrane lipids.

Rhodopsin 3

The hydrophilic sidechains point toward the interior of the protein.

Rhodopsin 4

Here is a water-soluble protein with five alpha helices.

Rhodopsin 5

In this case the hydrophobic sidechains point to the interior of the protein.

Rhodopsin 6

The hydrophilic residues point outward toward the solvent.
