Introduction

These scripts show some examples of alpha domain proteins.


Glucagon

Although the α-helix is a common structural element in proteins, it is rare to find a protein consisting of a single helix, because they are not usually stable. An exception is glucagon, shown here. (Based on 1gcn.pdb, Sasaki et al. (1975) Nature 257: 751-757)

Coiled Coil

The coiled-coil consists of two α-helices stacked together. In this case, the coils interact via leucines in the leucine zipper motif found in DNA-binding proteins. (Based on 1c94.pdb, Mittl et al. (2000) Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA 97: 2562-2566)

Spin On/Off

Four Bundle

A four bundle helix is a common way for α-helices to pack in a protein. (Based on 1fha.pdb, Lawson et al. (1991) Nature 349: 541-544)
