Pancreatic Trypsin Inhibitor

Introduction

These scripts show the complex between the pancreatic trypsin inhibitor (PTI) and trypsin. PTI is a small protein, Mr = 8 kDa. PTI binds to trypsin with high affinity because it contains a peptide bond that is a transition state analog. (Based on 2ptc.pdb, Marquart et al. (1983) Acta Crystallogr.,Sect.B 39: 480)

PTI 1

This is the complex between PTI and Trypsin.

PTI 2

A Lys of the inhibitor binds in the specificity pocket of trypsin.

PTI 3

The peptide bond between the Lys and Ala is distorted, which forces the carbonyl oxygen of the peptide bond into the oxyanion hole.

PTI 4

The peptide bond involving the Lys is non planar and resembles the reaction transition state - this inhibitor is a natural transition state analog.
