G-Actin

Introduction

These scripts show the structure of G-actin with ATP bound to the active site. (Based on 1atn.pdb, W. Kabach et al. (1990) Nature 347, 37-44.)

G-Actin 1

This script shows monomeric G-actin with ATP bound at the active site. A Ca2+ is also bound at the active site. G-actin hydrolyzes ATP ONLY during filament formation and NOT during muscle contraction.

G-Actin 2

This script illustrates the orientation in which ATP is bound to the protein. Only the triphosphate moiety of ATP is sequestered in the active site.
